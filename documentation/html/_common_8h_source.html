26  /*The hash multiplication method:
27  - Multiply the key by a constant A, 0 < A < 1,
28  - Extract the fractional part of the product,
29  - Multiply this value by m.
30 
31  Thus the hash function is:
32  h(k) = floor(m * (kA - floor(kA)))
33 
34  In this case, the value of m is not critical and we typically choose a power of 2 so that we can get the following efficient procedure on most digital computers:
35  - Choose m = 2p.
36  - Multiply the w bits of k by floor(A * 2w) to obtain a 2w bit product.
37  - Extract the p most significant bits of the lower half of this product.
38 
39  It seems that:
40  A = (sqrt(5)-1)/2 = 0.6180339887 -> KNUTH_NUMBER
41  is a good choice (see Knuth, "Sorting and Searching", v. 3 of "The Art of Computer Programming").*/
42  #define KNUTH_NUMBER 0.6180339887 //(sqrt(5)-1)/2
